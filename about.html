About

About

Engineer, researcher, and a seeker of patterns in the random world.

Researcher in computational mechanics with a core focus on the structural mechanics of heterogeneous materials. This space documents a research journey through the lens of stochastic modeling and uncertainty quantification, bridging the gap between applied mathematics, scientific computing, and engineering mechanics.

I build digital twins to visualize how uncertainty shapes the built environment, prioritizing a deep understanding of the "why" behind structural failure over the simple application of building codes. From random fields and Karhunen–Loève expansions to finite element simulations and artificial intelligence, the technical insights shared here are grounded in rigorous theory, experimental data, and open-source code.

Driven by a commitment to clarity and reproducibility, I share work involving stochastic FEM and scientific Python. I hope these explorations of advanced topics spark your curiosity and contribute to our shared path of continuous learning.

Everything on this site is grounded in rigorous theory, experimental data, and open-source code.

Engineering

My core domain is the structural safety of masonry—from the microscopic variations in a single brick to the reliability of massive historical walls.

I model, simulate, and analyze. My focus is on non-destructive testing, Bayesian data recovery, and probabilistic finite element analysis. My research applies these methods to unreinforced masonry: connecting the natural, messy variability of ancient materials to the high-stakes world of modern structural assessment.

I don’t just run simulations. I work to understand the physical reality they represent.

Before diving into deep numerical strategies, I spent time in the field. Measuring the pulse of materials with ultrasonic tools and seeing how real-world structures age teaches you things a computer model never will. It’s about bridging the gap between the laboratory and the street.

Programming

I specialize in scientific computing and automation, primarily using Python and specialized finite element solvers like CAST3M.

I write code that acts as a bridge between raw data and structural insight. Whether it’s building deep neural network surrogates that predict wall behavior in milliseconds or writing Bayesian algorithms to fill in missing experimental gaps, I focus on code that is transparent and reproducible.

In an era of black-box AI, I believe in explainable models. I use tools like SHAP to look under the hood of machine learning, ensuring that when a model makes a prediction, we actually understand the physics driving it.
