Where uncertainty meets simulation — one documented method at a time

Hello!!!!! I'm Abayomi (PhD), an independent researcher working at the intersection of Computational Mechanics, Stochastic Modeling, and Artificial Intelligence. My work focuses on how we model the unavoidable randomness in real engineering materials and structures — from quantifying spatial variability through variograms and covariance matrices, to propagating that uncertainty through finite element simulations using tools like the Karhunen–Loève expansion. This blog is my open notebook. Instead of keeping these methods buried in thesis chapters, I document them here in a way that is precise, accessible, and material-agnostic — useful whether you work with concrete, composites, metals, or geomaterials. Every post is grounded in real data and working Python code. If you are a student, researcher, or engineer curious about stochastic simulation, uncertainty quantification, or the computational side of materials, you are in the right place.
