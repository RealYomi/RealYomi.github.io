Research

My research focuses on the mathematical and computational foundations of uncertainty in engineering systems. Key areas of interest include:

Stochastic Modeling of Material Properties

Real materials exhibit spatial variability due to microstructural heterogeneity. I work on modeling Young’s modulus and other material parameters as random fields using covariance functions, variograms, and spectral decompositions.

Karhunen–Loève Expansion (KLE)

KLE provides a reduced-order representation of random fields. I explore its applications in stochastic finite element analysis, model reduction, and simulation of heterogeneous media.

Computational Mechanics

I apply numerical methods—finite elements, spectral methods, and mesh-based discretizations—to solve problems involving structural behavior, elasticity, and material uncertainty.

Uncertainty Quantification (UQ)

My work includes probabilistic modeling, sensitivity analysis, and quantifying the impact of randomness on engineering predictions.
